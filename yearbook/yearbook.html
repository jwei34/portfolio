Introduction

As Executive Editor for Brown University's yearbook, Liber Brunensis, I have done a variety of graphic design to produce my college's annual memo for the graduating class. Most of my work has involved using Adobe InDesign for layout design, but working on other elements of the book such as the cover and dividers has also involved using Illustrator, Photoshop, and Procreate.

2022 Book Layout Design Highlights

For the 2022 yearbook, I focused on the Timeline and Academics sections. The Timeline section was an idea our team came up with during COVID and aims to highlight important events that took place during the graduating class' undergraduate years, often involving the curation of excerpts from our student publications. The Academics section is often challenging to put together because of the lack of viisual content compared to other sections, but I aimed to make up for this by creating interesting data visualiztions and collecting statements and quotes from departments and students.

2021 Layout Design Highlights

For most of the 2021 book, we only had 3 layout editors on the team. As lead editor, I ended up designing roughly half of our 280-page book, i.e. almost 140 pages. We had to get creative with the how we collected our photos, relying on self-submissions (including Zoom screenshots and even memes), photos of seniors from previous years, and some of our own contributions—the first 2 spreads are composed of my own photography. Sections I worked on include Student Life, Timeline, Athletics, Organizations.
