Rental & Roomate Finder: Designing a Website based on a Startup Concept

Introduction •  Low-Fi Sketches •  High-Fi Mockups •  Post-Critique Iteration •  User Testing

Introduction

The goal of this project was to design an interactive interface around the concept of an emerging startup, working in a team of four to create multiple iterations of low- and high-fidelity mockups.

Startup Context

Kopa is the marketplace for monthly rentals and compatible housemates, and the company’s mission is to enable people to browse rentals, find roommates, and book and pay for spaces in a one-stop-shop. Currently the start-up is still in operation, and that’s why we believe a redesign might bring some real-world impact to this company.

Our favourite quote from the company founder and CEO is: “We believe that exploration and community make people whole. Kopa exists to inspire people to get out of their comfort zones, to form lasting relationships, and to live more fulfilled lives.”

Critique Session (ft. Industry expert!)

During our critique session, we presented the first iteration of our hi-fi mockups (see above). We had the opportunity to receive feedback on from not only fellow peers but also an industry expert, Gifford Cheung, who is a Sr. UX Researcher @Nintendo (and ex-classmate of Jeff!). Below, we highlighted some of the feedback we received from peers and Gifford.

General Feedback

Reviewers generally liked our UI (e.g. color palette) and design hierarchy for the 3 pages (sign-up, browse rentals, find roommates). Particularly, they thought the layouts of the sign-up and find roommates pages were fun and innovative.

Find Roommates Page Feedback

Suggestion #1: The page is a bit overwhelming without any guidance; clarification about its structure/algorithm would be helpful.

What we changed: Added welcome text briefly explaining our matching algorithm and categories. This also added some white space for improved hierarchy.

Suggestion #2: At first, it is unclear that users can scroll horizontally in addition to the traditional vertical scroll.

What we changed: Added arrows on the right end of each category to indicate the ability to scroll horizontally.

Browse Rentals Page Feedback

Suggestion #1: Reviewers thought our ‘Bookmarked’ box at the bottom right was an important component but wasn’t prominent enough.

What we changed: Made the ‘Bookmarked’ box larger, expandible/collapsible, and added a dark header section that pops out from the map more. We also added a shortcut to bookmarked items in the header, so it is accessible at all times.

Suggestion #2: Reviewers thought it'd be helpful to provide information about what kinds of information they can enter into the search bar.

What we changed: Added placeholder text to indicate possible entries for the area search.

User Testing

We gave users the following scenario, which we thought would encapsulate Kopa’s typical persona and user motivation:

  • Imagine you are a college student who has an internship in NYC this upcoming summer.
  • You want to compare short-term rentals within your price range.
  • You also want to find quiet, outdoorsy roommates you can share your lease with.
We also made sure to let users know that this is not an actual website but an interactive mockup made on prototyping software, so not all button functions are built out.

Tasks & Results

We gave users 3 tasks, which we thought would encapsulate the 3 key functionalities and screens of our website. Each task was assessed according to the following 2 metrics, “Success” (Yes or No) and “Difficulty” (Very difficult to Very easy, with a 5-point rating scale)

Task 1: Sign up as a renter. (Sign Up Page)
  • Users liked that they could indicate important preferences in the signup page, and saw that this would help inform Kopa’s roommate matching algorithm. This was in line with our expectations and even surprised us that they wouldn’t prefer more questions asking about their preferences and living habits.
Task 2: Find 2 rentals within a price range of $500-2,500/month. (Browse Rentals Page)
  • Despite our disclaimer that buttons are not fully built-out, users complained that they couldn’t fully use the ‘Price’ dropdown and ‘Sort’ buttons. This resulted in one of the users stating that they couldn’t complete the task assigned. Perhaps, we should have done a clearer job explaining what could be done or built out those specific functions in our prototype better.
  • No one used the expandible bookmarked box. We think that this was likely because the save listing functionality wasn’t built out, as our intention was that saving a listing would automatically add it to their bookmarked items and expand the box.
Task 3: Find roommates that are quiet and like the outdoors. (Find Roommates Page)
  • Feedback was overall positive; users thought it was easy to browse contact cards and complete their task of finding roommates with specific interests and habits.
  • One user noted that the writing was a bit small. This is a valid accessibility issue we would address if/when building out the actual website.

User Testing Summary & Potential Changes

Overall, the users thought our design was pretty intuitive to use and had a positive experience. They also liked its 2-in-1 function of browsing rentals and finding roommates. Some ​potential interface changes​​ we would make based on your UserTesting results and feedback include:

  • Increasing text size in listings & roommate cards for better accessibility and readability
  • Reconsidering the format and placement of ‘Bookmarked’ box for increased visibility, perhaps closer to the listings column.
